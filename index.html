Process Steps

1

Application Assignment

UWS PGR Admissions assigns applicants to PGR Lead (Dr Emma Reid) via Banner system. The PGR Lead then assigns to appropriate Division coordinator on Banner.

2

Supervision Team Formation

Division coordinator identifies potential supervision team based on:

  • PURE profile
  • Advice from HoD/ line manager/ UoA leads/ Research Centres and Groups

Division coordinator seeks approval to allocate applicant to potential Lead Supervisor (also called Director of Studies on Banner).

3

Proposal Review

Lead Supervisor is assigned applicant on Banner and reviews Proposal. Lead Supervisor works with Division PGR coordinator to identify second supervisor and Assessor.

4

Interview Arrangement

Lead Supervisor arranges interview with applicant on MS Teams (with PGR coordinator in attendance as Chair). Lead Supervisor emails applicant for interview.

Note: Lead Supervisor may also reject proposal or ask for revised proposal for interview. London applicants should have at least one supervisor on London campus.

5

Decision Process

Interview team makes a decision after the interview and informs the applicant by email. Lead Supervisor completes decision on Banner, including interview summary and other required information.

6

Approval Chain

The decision is submitted for approval by the Director of Studies, who then allocates the application to the Dean of School (Please select Dr Emma Reid as PGR Lead for approval). Once approved, it is submitted to PGR Admissions.

Research Proposal Requirements

Overview of Proposal Requirements

When applying for a PhD in the School of Business & Creative Industries, applicants are expected to provide a research proposal of between 1500-2000 words (excluding reference list).

Proposed Title

The proposed title of the research should include sufficient information to inform the reader as to the focus of the work, perhaps its conceptual framing and context. A good title will also help identify potential supervisors for doctoral studies.

Introduction (circa 500 words)

In the introduction, applicants should provide a clear background and justification for the study, including why it is important and what the research gap they're planning to address is. Normally, the introduction would also outline the overarching research aim and some research objectives (and/or research questions) that guide the study. When making the case for the proposed research, applicants can be expected to draw upon both academic and grey literatures.

Literature Review (circa 1000 words)

In a research proposal, reviewing the existing literature relating to the topic helps to isolate the research gap that will form the basis of the original contribution to knowledge. Though there will be limitations as to what can be covered in a research proposal, this is an opportunity for applicants to demonstrate their knowledge of the field and their ability to synthesise and distil a larger body of literature into a concise form. It will help potential supervisors to assess how knowledgeable applicants are on their chosen topic while illustrating their critical thinking and writing skills. Applicants may complete their literature review by stating or restating the study's research questions.

Proposed Research Methodology (circa 500 words)

This section should detail how applicants will go about addressing their research questions in the form of an empirical investigation or approach to creative practice (this will depend on the field of study). Applicants are expected to outline the overarching methodological approach and detail the methods they expect to employ. The school recognizes that applicants may not have every element of their methodology worked out but this section provides the reader with the opportunity to assess their understanding of research strategy and design and how realistic their data collection methods are.

References

Research proposals should include a full list of references used to inform the research proposal. These should be provided using the Harvard referencing system.

Interview Process

Sample Email to Applicant

Dear [Applicant Name],

We are pleased to inform you that your application for the PhD position within the UWS School of Business and Creative Industries has been considered, and we are pleased to advance it to the next stage.

Your proposed topic on [research topic] aligns with our school's research focus, and we believe it will make a significant contribution to the field.

We would like to extend an invitation for a formal interview via MS Teams during the week commencing [date]. Kindly inform us of the dates and times that you will be available.

During the interview, you will engage in a conversation with the panel, which should last approximately 30 minutes.

The panel will consist of:

  • [Name], [Title]
  • [Name], [Title]
  • [Name], [Title]
  • [Name], Postgraduate Research Lead

If you have any specific requirements related to the interview or your prospective PhD application, please notify us at your earliest convenience. This will enable us to discuss any necessary equipment or facilities you may need. Our commitment is to ensure the process is as equitable as possible for you.

Thank you and we look forward to hearing from you.

Best regards,

[Lead Supervisor Name]

During the Interview

  • Lead Supervisor and Second supervisor may ask questions about topic, experience, methodology etc.
  • PGR coordinator will ask about understanding of PGR journey, why UWS, timelines, funding.
  • Applicant may also ask questions.
  • Interview team to make a decision after the interview and inform the applicant by email.

Post-Interview Decision Process: Complete Decision Slip on Banner

Decision Slip

Lead Supervisor completes decision on Banner, including interview summary and confirming it took place on Teams. Select unconditional offer unless there is missing document.

Verify Requirements

Confirm English language, research group, campus, subject area, and other details.

Confirmation

Select YES for ethics approval, confirm funding, and complete supervisory arrangements.

Submit Decision

Save all changes and submit the decision for approval.

In Approval Status, select approve and then under comment write – approved. Then “Your role” – select Director of Studies. Next approver should then be Dr Emma Reid, who will approves it and submits to PGR Admissions.

Enrollment Process

PGR Student Intake

There are 4 intakes per year on Scottish campuses:

  • October
  • February
  • April
  • June

Note: CAS for international applicants usually takes 6-8 weeks.

Post-Interview Admissions Process

Admissions approves application and sends offer letter

Enrollment opens and student invited to Enroll

BCI Doctoral team organise UWS laptop and email/internet access once student has enrolled

PGR (Doctoral College) set up Skillsforge account for applicant and allocate supervisory team

PGR arrange induction event

BCI PGR coordinators arrange School induction and add student to BCI PGR Teams site

Supervisors encouraged to meet with student at induction time and then meet minimum once per month
